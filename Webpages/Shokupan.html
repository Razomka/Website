Tasty and Sweet, The Shokupan Loaf!

A Little History Of Shokupan And Tips On Making It

Or if you want to jump straight to the recipe! Been here, read that.

If you are planning on making this. Be wary that the yudane should be done the night before.

Shokupan is a delicious, soft, sweet and fluffy white bread from Japan. It is said to orginate from the 1880s made by a British baker, but is highly different from our regular loaves. This is due to the inclusion of milk and a special but simple ingredient called a yudane.

A yudane is just simply bread flour mixed with either water or milk and heated to around 65°C and this makes the starch more gelatinious. This is then torn up and added to a simple white loaf recipe. This allows the loaf to become extra fluffy but also adds a slight sweetness, combine this with milk, you end up with an excellent loaf.

I've included the baker's percentages below and I would like to mention that the yudane is 20% of the flour, so if you are using 500g of flour then the yudane will be 100g and your flour for the loaf will be 400g.

Now that you know a little about it, lets start with the recipe.

First It Begins With The Yudane

Ingredients

Shokupan in a 900g sized tin

The Yudane

The yudane should be done the NIGHT BEFORE or 8-12 HOURS BEFORE.

  • 100g Strong White Flour
  • 100ml Boiling Water
  1. Pop your strong white flour into a mixing bowl.
  2. Add your boiled water and then mix together until it comes together as a ball.
  3. Cover the bowl with some cling film and then either leave aside or pop into the fridge for at least 8 hours.

And that's step one of the recipe. Not very hard but very important.

Onto the Shokupan Loaf

Be sure to have the yudane done prior!

Ingredients

Continuing our Shokupan Loaf

  • 400g Strong White Flour
  • 30g Sugar
  • 6g Instant Dried Yeast
  • 10g Salt
  • 20g Butter
  • 250ml Lukewarm Milk
  1. Get your ingredients ready including the yudane made yesterday or at least 8 hours ago.
  2. Sift the flour into a large mixing bowl.
  3. Add the salt & sugar to one side and the yeast to the other side.
  4. Add the yudane by tearing it into small pieces and adding to your flour.
  5. Add your butter and then slowly add the milk while mixing, you can use a spoon or your hands.
  6. When the ingredients start to come together, form it into a very rough ball.
  7. Set aside your mixing bowl and spread a small handful of flour over where you shall knead your dough.
  8. Remove the dough from the mixing bowl and place onto the floured worktop and start kneading. Knead for at least 10mins.
  9. When the dough begins to form a smooth outer skin, try the windowpane test.
  10. Add a little oil to a new bowl and place your kneaded dough into it.
  11. Add a dish cloth or something similar over the top and leave the bowl in a warm area for at least an hour.
  12. After the hour, flour your worktop and add a little oil or butter to your loaf tin.
  13. Remove the dough from the bowl and on your worktop, fold it onto itself until you have removed the air within.
  14. Divide the dough into two or three pieces of equal weight. Leave this to rest under a cloth for 20mins.
  15. After 20mins, take the first piece and flatten it using a rolling pin into a loose rectangle, making sure two edges are longer than the other
  16. Roughly measure the width of your loaf tin. Our flatten dough will have the same width
  17. We do this by rotating the dough so that the shortest edge is closest to you and tightly folding the long edges into the middle making sure that the dough has a similar length to your tin
  18. Now tightly roll up the loaf starting at the closest edge to you like a swiss roll
  19. Place this seam side down into your prepared loaf tin on one side
  20. Repeat this step for the other pieces and placing those onto the other side then one in the middle(if you did 3 pieces)
  21. Cover with the dish cloth and leave for 30mins
  22. Before the 30min mark, pre-heat your oven to 185°C (fan-over) or 200°C Convential, you'll know yourself how long this takes. Mine takes 10 minutes
  23. Remove the dish cloth and if you feeling fancy, you can brush some beaten egg over the top.
  24. Bake the loaf for 25-30mins
  25. Check the loaf after 25-30mins and see if the top is a lovely golden brown then remove it.
  26. Take the loaf out of the tin and tap the bottom to check if it sounds hollow, if it does then rest it on a cooling rack. If it doesn't then place back into the oven without the tin for a few more minutes, I usually go for 7-10mins
  27. After cooling, cut it into large thick slices and eat it as you please!

The baker's precentages for this recipe

As shokupan is usually made within a tin, it is very useful to know how to scale it up or down to fit into different tins. Remember that with this recipe, the yudane is 20% of your total flour and the rest is used to be mixed with the milk.
