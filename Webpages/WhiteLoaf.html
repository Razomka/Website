The Little White Loaf That Could!

These Are My Handy Little Tips Prior To The Recipe

Or if you want to jump straight to the recipe! Been here, read that.

I'll have these tips throughout my recipes as I feel there is always something important to mention and I don't want to clog up the recipe with random information or tidbits.

For this white loaf, while a basic recipe, it does require you to knead the dough and shape the dough. These are important steps but easy to learn.

If you are unfamiliar with kneading and shaping. I would like to redirect you to this page here. This page contains both instructions and videos on how to knead and shape your bread.

There isn't really anything to else to say as the recipe is nice and basic. So I'll talk about an useful concept called baker's percentages.

Baker's percentages

I only recently learnt about these and they are very useful, particularly for bread making, and not too difficult to understand!

The way they work is that you break down the components into percentages against the main ingredient. In our case, this would be strong white flour. Might not make sense for now, but bear with me.

My loaf below uses 400g of strong white flour. So that is 100%. Now I use 260ml of water, so to figure out the percentage of water to flour.

It would be as follows: 260/400 * 100 = 65% Water.

After getting the percentages of each ingredient. You can start using these percentages to upscale the size of your loaf, while maintaining the same texture as before!

Want to make 3 x 300g loaves? Then we'll need 900g flour, then 65% of 900g to get our water volume. Voila! Now you can make any loaf of any size!

I'll include the baker's percentages into all my baking recipes if you ever want to ever scale up or down with the ingredients.

Ingredients

White Loaf in a 900g loaf tin

  • 400g Strong White Flour
  • 7g Instant Dried Yeast
  • 7g Salt
  • 30g Butter
  • 260ml Lukewarm Water

Instructions

  1. Get your ingredient ready.
  2. Sift the flour into a large mixing bowl.
  3. Add the salt to one side and the yeast to the other side.
  4. Add your butter and then slowly add water while mixing, you can use a spoon or your hands.
  5. When the ingredients start to come together, form it into a very rough ball in the mixing bowl.
  6. Set aside your mixing bowl and spread a small handful of flour over where you shall knead your dough.
  7. Remove the dough from the mixing bowl and place onto the floured worktop and start kneading. Knead for 5-10mins.
  8. When the dough begins to form a smooth outer skin, try the windowpane test.
  9. Add a little oil to a new bowl and place your kneaded dough into the bowl.
  10. Add a dish cloth or something similar over the top and leave the bowl in a warm area for at least an hour.
  11. After the hour, flour your worktop, remove the dough from the bowl and on your worktop, fold it onto itself until you have removed the air within.
  12. Shape the dough.
  13. Add a little oil or butter to your loaf tin and add the shaped dough to it.
  14. Cover with the dish cloth and leave for 30mins.
  15. Before the 30min mark, pre-heat your oven to 200°C Fan Oven or 220°C Conventional, you'll know yourself how long this takes. Mine takes 10mins.
  16. Remove the dish cloth and do a few slashes across the top of the loaf with a sharp knife and then pop into the oven for 30-40mins.
  17. Check the loaf after 30-40mins and see if the top is a lovely golden brown then remove it.
  18. Take the loaf out of the tin and tap the bottom to check if it sounds hollow, if it does then rest it on a cooling rack. If it doesn't then place back into the oven without the tin for a few more minutes, I usually go for 7-10mins.
  19. After cooling, enjoy it with some tasty butter or your favourite spreads! You can also toast it and it will taste great.

The Baker's Percentages For This Recipe

For my recipe, I used the following percentages. Useful if you want to scale up to a larger loaf or multiple loaves.

Ingredient Percentage
Strong White Flour 100%
Lukewarm Water 65%
Butter 7.5%
Salt 1.75%
Yeast 1.75%
